MARCH 2020 路 SRIRANGAPATNA, INDIA

I was 17 years old
when the pandemic hit.

This is the story of how I built India's first Kannada COVID-19 symptom checker in 72 hours.

SCROLL
MARCH 27, 2020

I was sitting at home in Srirangapatna, watching the news. Karnataka had just become India's third most COVID-affected state.

Government helplines were overwhelmed. People were panicking. And I noticed something that bothered meevery symptom checker I could find was in English or Hindi.

60M
Kannada speakers with no symptom checker in their language

60 million people. Invisible to the pandemic response.

My father is a health inspector. I grew up understanding how critical clear health communication isespecially in someone's native language. If you can't understand the questions, how can you know if you're at risk?

THAT SAME NIGHT

I decided to build it myself.

No fancy AI. No machine learning. Just a simple, practical symptom checker that anyone could usein Kannada. I called my friend Pranav at the University of Maryland. He was in.

72
hours from idea to live product
MARCH 28-30, 2020

Three days of barely sleeping. Writing code. Translating medical terms. Testing. Fixing. Testing again.

We used Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for the design. Deployed on DigitalOcean with help from Unbox Robotics for enterprise infrastructure. We knew if this worked, we'd get traffic. We had to be ready.

MARCH 31, 2020 路 12:00 PM

We hit publish.

COVIDSELFCHECK.APP went live. India's first COVID-19 symptom diagnostic app in Kannada. I shared it on social media and waited.

10 HOURS LATER

The Deputy Chief Minister of Karnataka went on live television and asked to integrate our app into the state's official helpline.

I was watching TV with my family when it happened. My phone started blowing up. Friends, relatives, strangerseveryone saw it.

We made it open source from day one.

The code was public before the app was famous. We didn't use machine learning or AIwe didn't need to. This was about practicality, not impressing anyone. Just simple, clear questions in someone's own language.

The pandemic is over now.
The app served its purpose.

But I'll never forget those 72 hours. What it felt like to see something I built actually help people. To see strangers become teammates overnight.

Open source. Community-driven. No fancy AI. Just people helping people.
